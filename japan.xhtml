History

1868-1941, Meiji Restoration, Fukoku kyōhei, Manchuria, Militarism, Biological Weapons, The Fall of China and Pearl Harbor

Japanese Era of Technocentric Fanatical Militarism

DN-1877, Japan-Joseon Gunboat Treaty (RL-1876, Japan-Korea Treaty of Amity/ Treaty of Ganghwa Island) occurred similar to what happened in reality. However, an extra article was added, Article 11, which permitted the Japanese to build and maintain a naval base in Cholla. This set a precedent for a much more thorough "reeducation" or enslavement of the peninsula in the future.

Catalyst to the first Sino-Japanese War. DN-1894-1895, Donghak Peasant Revolution catalysed the Japanese annexation of Korea/Joseon as happened in RL-1894. With a pro-Japanese government formed in Korea by the Japanese insertion force.

DN-1894-1896, First Sino-Japanese War (RL-1894-1895), though the fighting came to a stop in 1895 after the Treaty of Shimonoseki on 17th of April 1895, Japanese military units formed a subdivision of research units tasked in weapon development, using kidnapped Chinese sailors, and prisoners of war as lab rats. Many such bases were built across mainland Asia over the course of a few decades before the start of the second world war. At these camps, they managed to create many amalgamations and monstrous biological weapons through inhumane experiments. These experiments continued until the end of World War II, and many created monsters were deployed by the Japanese.

DN-1910, Japan-Korea Treaty, following the annexation of Korea post-treaty, the Japanese created an army comprised of conscripted Koreans, comprised mostly out of criminals and political prisoners comprised of both men and women, they were designated as a corps of suicide bombers used to bum-rush enemy fortifications. These men and women weren't loyal, however, they were threatened with the lives of their family members, not knowing that those family members are or will also meet the same fate, the children of these men and women are kept in camps to be "reeducated" or brainwashed into soldiers of the same task. Meanwhile, other Koreans, were conscripted into the army or forced to work in labour camps to create armaments and other essentials for the Japanese war machine. All of this in the hopes of achieving Fukoku kyōhei (Rich Nation, Strong Army)

In RL-1936, on February 26 a Japanese army unit attempted a coup d'etat to overthrow Civilian rule, this failed, however the aftermath of it was an increased military budget for the army and navy in order to appease and hopefully quench the thirst of the warmongers. However, this occurred much quicker in the world of DOMINION. In DN-1931, following the Mukden Incident (or the Manchuria Incident), the Japanese Invasion of Manchuria occurred, and the following year, DN-1932, Manchukuo (Manchoukou)- the Japanese puppet was not formed as it happened in RL-1932, instead it was fully annexed as the Japanese did in RL-1910 with Korea.

DN1937, Second Sino-Japanese War, (RL-1937, the war ended with world war II with the Japanese surrender.). In DOMINION, the second Sino-Japanese War was won by the Japanese rapidly. In DN-1938, following the Japanese takeover of Wuhan, motions to put the amalgamations they have been experimenting on since the first Sino-Japanese War gained popularity, and so, the Japanese Imperial Army deployed the Yōkai Gundan (Yokai Army Corps), armed with 3 units created from the biological experiments. The Akaname, The Oni, and The Kappa. They were first seen in combat at the 2nd Battle of Changsha. The unit managed to completely destroy the Chinese forces in Changsha through sheer brutality. The bioweapon units, resembled much of their looks from Yōkai legends. With poison gas, and Yōkai Gundan, the Japanese army managed to wipe out the Chinese army in Changsha, sending the remnants running inland, and successfully annexed the most of China with the surrender of the Government in Chongqing on December 31st, DN-1941. Though there is no formal government commanding the armies of the CCP and KMT, guerillas and resistance group continued fighting until the end of World War II, with significant efforts trying to sabotage Japanese bases across the coasts. However these more often failed, with only one significant win by Chinese forces in the 7th Battle of Changsha where Chinese forces managed to take down multiple Oni units with bazookas supplied by the Americans.

"60,000 Chinese soldiers were torn apart by demons released by the Japanese." An American observer noted in his report.

Hostilities between the Japanese and the Americans finally reached boiling point during the attack at Pearl Harbor. On December 7, DN-1941, Japanese forces attacked Pearl Harbor. The Japanese sunk all eight ships docked at the harbor at the time before gassing the Oahu with poison gas, and dropping multiple Oni units onto the island. The attack ended the lives of 43,578 American soldiers and sailors, and injuring the 23,211 others, leaving less than 1/4 of the original military personnel stationed there able to fight. Japan later announced a declaration of war towards the United States, followed by Germany and Italy (despite no formal obligation stated in the tripartite act).

1941-1951, Japanese Invasion and Occupation of Indochina, Dutch East Indies, Malaya, Singapore, North Borneo, Brunei, and Crown Colony of Labuan

The Second World War ended in 1950 and Malaya, Singapore, North Borneo, Brunei, and Crown Colony of Labuan was surrendered to the British in 1951.

1951-1955, The Great Cleansing

Allied Forces initiative to clean up the biological waste and remnant abominations across the world

1951-1999, British and American Rule in Japan

Following the surrender and execution of the Japanese Royal family, British Forces and American Forces colonised Japan

2000-2006, Japanese fight for independence

In 2006, the formation of the Japanese Free State was established, Japan was not allowed to have their own fighting force, and is only allowed to maintain a coast guard of 100 patrol boats and 9 frigates.

2006-2043, Indentured Peace

Technology

The Japanese war machine, despite being leagues more advanced than the allies and the Germans in the field of Biotechnology was falling behind in terms of creating conventional armaments and vehicles. Despite having built 2 Yamato Battleships, the Yamato and the Yamato II, the IJA and IJN was severely deprived of reliable tanks, and other large engines. As is the case with mechanised infantry, their conventional arms were subpar.

Biotechnology

Following the conclusion of the First Sino-Japanese War, the Japanese Empire started to conduct small scale human experimentations on Chinese and Korean sailors, prisoners and kidnapped civilians. The experiments started simple through very crude organs transplantations. The earliest ever trial of human organ transplantation was conducted to two captured Chinese women where they had their kidneys swapped. This experiment was a failure, the patients died, and so does the next 3000 subjects. However they did finally manage to keep two subjects live for 3 months at the longest before the development of a much more sophisticated method of transplantation.

With the discovery of organ transplantations in the DN-1899, the Japanese researchers, who is now referred to as the Imperial Japanese Human Research Group (In English), conducted more inhumane researches, for example; combining twins together, combining humans with animal bodies, and putting live subjects conscious and living into jars. In 1902, the Japanese discovered a cancer cell that could mutate humans to weird proportions. They used this cancer cell to develop the first ever "Oni", this Oni died within 36 hours of its life, it was said that the human subject used was conscious during the entirety of the 34 hours and 29 minutes of its life as an Oni. This failure made the Japanese obsessed with developing human weapons.

In 1910, after the annexation of Korea, the Japanese found a peculiar substance buried deep within the capital city, this substance was later dubbed "Element K" by the allies after the second world war or "Sora" by the Japanese. This became the catalyst to the human experiments that carried on afterwards, creating the first Yōkai of the first series of Yōkai Gundan by 1919. Starting with the completed Oni (1919), a large humanoid creature crafted to be a mindless glutton that would eat any humans nearby, and then the Akaname(1923), a human sized humanoid carnivore that has a large tongue used to choke and grab victims, and the Kappa(1927), a specialised water insertion creature that is used to destabilise bridges. The Kappa was the only Yōkai that could be controlled by the Japanese through training, all other relied on smell repellents to avoid friendly fire.

The Yōkai Gundan however was not made a full fledged unit in the Japanese Army until after Manchuria was annexed. Following the success of the Yōkai Gundan in China, the project was expanded and many more Chinese, and Koreans being used in the experiments. These abominations possess very high strength and are hard to kill, however, each one can be neutralised by the unit who deployed it through a serum that is shot or injected into their body, these serums will stop their heart, instantly killing them. However, by the end of the second world war, many of these units remain operational due to a genetic mutation that made them immune to the serums and allow them to reproduce.

Mechanised Infantry

Japanese mechanised infantry units were subpar when compared to allied made mechanised infantry units and German infantry units. The only reliable and common unit seen in action throughout the war is the "Type 40 Suicider", first deployed in 1941 in the attack of Malaya. Despite the name, it was mostly used for logistics of heavy materials and convoy defence. The suicider designation is due to it's self-destruct feature. The pilot operating the Type 40 Suicider is more often welded into the machine before a "suicide" operation.

Disposables

The Imperial Japanese Army utilisation of suicide bombers were common. One particular unit is the "Bakudan Chosenjin", a corps comprised of Koreans used as suiciders against their will. Another similar unit is the "Chankoro", these are Chinese prisoners used as combat engineers, they are to dig trenches and build roads where they will spend their entire lives at, after construction is finished, 10% of the best in the unit is spared and sent to a different unit and the rest will be buried alive, drowned or burned, depending on whichever is the most efficient.
