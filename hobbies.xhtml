Reading

Most people love to read novels, me, I'm a tad bit different, I like to read philosophy, history, and political works. To me, seeing the world in the eyes of another thinker is very fun. Opinions differ everywhere, some I could agree with, some don't. Whether to hate or to like, I must be able to understand them first. For example, I have read many philosophical works, and one of them happens to be the Prince by Niccolo Machiavelli, to me, a person who read the Prince (Il Principe), and immediately likes it should not be a leader of a nation, because that work was made as propaganda for the De Medici family. Later on in life, Machiavelli writes "Florentine Histories" which conflicts with many things he made not in the Prince.

Some say that I am a bit pompous with the books that I read. Here is the thing, if your favourite book is 50 Shades of Grey, and you like it because it was "profound", your opinions are invalid when it comes to books. Secondly, if your book suggestion to me for reading something written in Malay is "Legasi Pejuang 2" by Arman Jazee, you probably haven't read that many books.
