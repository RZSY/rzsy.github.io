Education Life

An Ode To Education

Some people would boast about their academic achievements, because academia, is a hard nut to grasp, crack and consume. Me, I will not boast about my academic achievements, partially because there is nothing to boast about (I am very well below average), and mainly because I believe that achievements in academia isn't really useful if you get stuck in a supermarket filled with angry panic-buyers in the middle of Bandaraya Kolumpo (Kuala Lumpur). I'm of course joking, education is very important. I took part in it as a student, once as a YT (Young Tutor) in SEMESRA for English (somehow).

I used to be an introvert in school, I like to claim that I still am, but people won't believe me.

To me, I vehemently believe that there is no "good" school, and "bad" school, to me, school is just a place to learn and gain knowledge. The building carrying a title of "kluster kecemerlangan", "Top 10", or whatever else has nothing to do with the student itself. There is no bad school. There is only bad students, and bad educators. Students who are bad are students who don't commit and do what they are supposed to do as students, which is studying, and educators who are bad are educators who don't do their task, which is to educate. The fact that a school is good or bad is not up to the labels, it is up to what those who partake in it make out of their experience there.

Primary Education

I started my formal, governmentally regulated education in Sekolah Kebangsaan Setiawangsa (WBA0058), SPBT Sekolah Kluster Kecemerlangan. It was a weird time of my life. I used to be so short and I get bullied a bunch by kids twice my size. I used to wonder how they managed to get that big as a pre-pubescent child, what did their parents feed them? other pre-pubescent children? Animal growth steroids? They were so large, and often they are as large as the PE teacher (Physical Education, PJK in Malay, Pendidikan Jasmani dan Kesihatan), Cikgu Azeze (pronounced Ah-Zee-Zee), by adult standards he's not large, but by the standards of a 7 year old boy with spikey Chinese looking hair, he's quite big. I forgot the names of most of my bullies in this period, but I do remember their faces. It is sad that they don't end up as roadkill in the evening RTM news, but to each their own fate I suppose. I do know one thing though, they were quite rich (probably how they got to that size, by having the money to buy other pre-pubescent children as lunch).

As a primary school student, my life was very weird. Saturday morning, I had archery, Saturday evening I had art class at MIA (Malaysian Institute of Art), Sunday morning I had rugby, and Sunday evening I had piano lessons at YAMAHA music school. I was busy as a child It was tiring. It was a weird time. I have lead a life where I have felt rich, poor, sick, tired and happy. I'd ask for a simple one, but we know it doesn't work like that.

Secondary Education

In 2019, I started studying in this "glorious" school called Sekolah Menengah Sains Rembau (NEA3037) or SEMESRA, which I love to call "SENGSARA" because it was simply hell on earth. Form 1 (one) started and I was dropped in the middle of nowhere Rembau alone. It was like being dropped in a hostile territory without any weapons, and armour. First day there and me and the other Form 1s (one) were called to a senior's room, KP10 (Kelana Petra 10), and there they regailed us with the rules of the establishment, not formal rules of the school, but the rules of seniority, which to this day I find stupid. There, I was bullied thoroughly, others would say that it's a normal occurrence in boarding schools, even my father said so, but I think that we as a society have failed as a whole if wrong that is normalised is considered Okay. There I had to keep a fake smile in front of the seniors when they force me to do anything, and honestly, I would murder them if not for were my size back then. The torture that I felt there I wouldn't wish upon others. I failed everything remotely academic except for English in that school.

The bullying became worse in March when Open Day was getting near. After Prep (Preparotory Classes 9PM - 11PM), we had rollcalls every night to paint and decorate the entire floor for Open Day, and often, we would finish at around 3.30AM. We have to wake up and get ready by 5.30AM. At first it was alright, but on the 5th day, one of us who were sleep deprived fell and spilled oil paint on the floor. That apparently warranted a torture session where we were told to do Moto (squatting with both hands in front) from 11PM all the way to 4AM. I didn't do it until 4AM because I fainted and was sent to a hospital. The wardens didn't do anything and covered for my seniors. That day I lost trust for wardens. A sane person would tell their parents about this, and I was sane. I suppose I was no longer sane after that. The year went on like that, do a small forgivable mistakes and we would be tortured in the name of "discipline". What made me really scared was the fact that everyone was brainwashed by the tortures. They see it as character building. Honestly, the only thing I got was trauma and a few years worth of depression.

In October that year, I lost a friend of mine there, his name was Bah, an Orang Asli Student. He was bullied badly that he was hospitalised. I pity him. I was hospitalised too, but his case was worse. He was placed in a ward. His case actually got covered by a news outlet.

The article I mentioned

Funny thing about all of this is the fact that even when one of us were thoroughly destroyed like that, none of them openly voiced out more. No one fought for him. I regret that. SEMESRA was not a good place. It can claim to be a good school, but to me that place was hell. I left that school during Form 4 (four). I enterred with hopes of becoming a doctor or lawyer, and I left with depression and the fact that I want to be a librarian, because there in that school there were only two safe places that I felt truly safe in, the PSS and with some of my friends, Ahmad Syahmi (Choomy), Dzuldanish Hussain(Zul), and one of the only good senior I had, Luqman Hakeem(Luke). We stuck around for a long while mostly because the others looked at us as a bunch of weirdos. The moment I left school, I felt my soul freed. Even though my family scolded me badly when I was expelled. Even though I was the prospective Pengurusi Pengawas Pusat Sumber Sekolah after being one from Form 1 to 4, I felt good leaving that place. Even in hell there were good people, like Cikgu Riyanti, and Madam Shima. My departure from Negeri Yang Di-Pertuan Besar Negeri Sembilan Duli Yang Maha Mulia Tuanku Muhriz Ibni Almarhum Tuanku Munawir was bittersweet.

End of Form 4 (four) I started schooling at Sekolah Menengah Kebangsaan Taman Setiawangsa, a bad school in the eyes of my parents. There I took Ekonomi, Perniagaan, Pendidikan Seni Visual and regular Science. I missed my friends back in hell, but it was better there for me. I took my SPM there and I got 1A+ 1A 1A- 4B+ and 2C. It was so much better than having no passes in SEMESRA.

Tertiery Education

As stated above, I'm currently in my 4th semester in Universiti Teknologi MARA, and right now I am somehow the class representative for KCDIM1444E. Weird, I know. How am I a class representative, I am very incompetent. I am also the Naib Timbalan Presiden of Bulan Sabit Merah Malaysia UiTM Kedah, somehow. It's so weird not gonna lie. It's like: "Look at this dude, he looks like a dude, give him a spot,", and the clerk be like "Ah aight luv, here you go!". I am the most irresponsible person to have ever set foot in this fine institution.
