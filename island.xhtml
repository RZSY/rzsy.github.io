The Man on the Iron Island

A Work In Progress

Have you ever woke up in the morning looking forward to seeing people that you hold so dearly, people you thought you could trust, people who in your eyes you couldn’t live without but despite all that loyalty and love for them, your expectations were just you being delusional?

All the things you thought about them were proven to be lies. Have you ever looked straight into the mirror and look at the face your grandmother described as handsome or pretty and the only thing you see staring back at you looked like an empty husk that looked uncannily human but not human enough to be loved?

If you answered no, you’re a lucky soul and I wish that you will never have the chance to feel it, and to those who have felt it before, one day that feeling will be a dusty memory on a shelf lined with all the bad things in life in a storage depot in your mind. Sure now it seems like that Golden Era is a millennia away from today, maybe to you by the time that happens you’ll be stuck on a wheelchair surrounded by ungrateful kids waiting for you to flop dead like a trout after getting evicted from their river home in order to lay their grubby little hands on your hard earned cash. Well, what do I know, I’m only 37.

My name is Chris, last name omitted because I do not want to be associated with a bunch of asses that made me who I am today, a pathetic soul of no notable achievements. Yet, somehow, every ex-girlfriend I had calls me a selfish narcissistic fool. I was born in the early years of the 2000s. My childhood was typical, it was uneventful for a child during the time, neglectful busy and in-the-process-of getting a divorce parents, taken cared by grandparents, watching Thomas the Tank Engine on the telly, all the usual stuff.

I am a librarian, not a doctor like my mum wanted, not an engineer like my dad wanted, just a librarian, who is in the middle of being phased out by artificial intelligence. Working a dead-end job in post-meridiem Earth, the moon is colonised by the British Empire and the Communists in China, Mars is the 61st state of New America, and Africa, is as always, riddled with political violence and civil wars. I sat on my chair looking into the shining screen. Despite the advancements in technology, we’re still using the Second Edition of the Anglo-American Cataloguing Rules, alongside RDA (Resource Description Access) of course. The chair I’m sitting on is government property, thus means that it will break if I touch the height stick thing wrong. I got up from my chair, my cup of coffee is empty, the library has a lot of people yet the ones who come here are never people that wants to read and learn, those who come here are young people trying to get air-conditioning, or couldn’t pay for a room to have *** in, and they never try to at least be quiet enough. ******** the lot of them.
