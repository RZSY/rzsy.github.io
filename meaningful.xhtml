Meaningful Education for a Nation’s Progress, Innovation and Inclusiveness

Essay by Rizq Syahmi Bin Muhammad Fuad for the 2025 Tengku Ahmad Rithauddeen Essay Writing Competition

“It is the very nature of life to strive to continue in being. Since this continuance can be secured only by constant renewals, life is a self-renewing process. What nutrition and reproduction are to physiological life, education is to social life.” John Dewey, 1930.

The advent of a new era always brings forth a trying time where the preparations of yesterday plays a role too huge for a person to carry without the proper training and guidance, and that proper training and guidance is education, meaningful education. However, the topic of meaningful education for a single person in a trying era is not the topic of this essay, instead the thematic that will be discussed is regarding a collective of people united under the flag of a nation. Education, in its roots, belongs under the umbrella of social sciences, and as mentioned above in the quote by John Dewey, education is the constant renewal of life. To actualise the title into our reality; meaningful education for a nation’s progress, innovation and inclusiveness, it is vital for a person to understand the definition and meaning behind it.

First and foremost, the simplification of the goals of this essay can be summarised as this; through the usage of meaningful education to achieve the goals of national progress, innovation and inclusiveness. The definition of meaningful education, according to the Oxford Learner’s Dictionary, meaningful is serious and important, and education is a process of teaching, training and learning, especially in schools, colleges or universities, to improve knowledge and develop skills, as for the term “meaningful education”, in the logical sense, can be defined as; the serious and important process of teaching, training and learning formally in an educational organisation. To define national progress, innovation, and inclusiveness, it is important to know in terms of what the definition should be. For the definition of national progress, the criteria that will be considered are Gross Domestic Product (GDP), unemployment rates and crime and incarceration rates. Next, Innovation can be measured through the increase in development in country through the usage of domestic assets and the decrease in import dependency. Lastly, inclusiveness can be defined in two ways, the diversification of domestic productivity across different sectors whilst providing equal opportunities for success towards all citizens of Malaysia without the separation of gender, race and religion, and the globalisation of Malaysian interests in a way that benefits the nation entirely and is according to the 17 Sustainable Development Goals (SDG) made by the United Nations (UN).

How is a meaningful education related to any of the listed benchmarks? Starting with the benchmarks for progress. Firstly, GDP, a nation’s GDP plays a vital role in attracting investors and tourists, how does a meaningful education contribute to the GDP is through the number of professionals born and raised in the nation; by producing more educated people, the nation is able to churn out a much more educated workforce to work the demands of the new world. Next, unemployment rates, the more educated a country is, the more jobs’ opportunities appear through the formation of new firms and companies, through the expansion of a government agency or body. Lastly, according to Lance Lochner (2012), “the role of education as a human capital investment that increases future legitimate work opportunities, which discourages participation in crime”, this meant that the more educated a person is, the more they understand the value of hard work rather than submitting to a life of crime. All of these can then also be related to the next two requirements of the essay, the innovation of the nation and the inclusiveness, can all be related to the three reasons as to why education is as important as it sounds. Innovation is something that happens through thinking, and to think in a mature and logical way, a person requires an education first, same with inclusiveness, to promote diversity in the nation itself, to provide a quality education and environment for all requires a thinking mind devoid of childish xenophobia and the best way to rid the world of childish and immature xenophobic thoughts is through education, and the globalisation of Malaysian interests, to increase the standings of our country in the eyes of the world and the global books of index and happiness, we need an education.

To realise these goals through meaningful education, it is essential that we must first address the weaknesses in the current system of education. Firstly, the problem of access. “Most children in Malaysia receive, 11 years of education, although there is a substantial number of out of school children. Adolescents are more at risk of dropping out of secondary school than younger children at primary school,” Kirsten Anderson and Dr. Justine Barrett (2020), the unavailability of access to education for them can be due to the costs required to send children to school and the accessibility of the education itself. The public schooling system has been made available by the Malaysian Government, particularly through the Ministry of Education (MOE) and Ministry of Higher Education (MOHE), but the fact that access to an education is still not available to many children in Malaysia, particularly in many rural areas in Sabah and Sarawak, and other small islands along the coasts of both the peninsula and Borneo. Secondly, the problem of quality of education. To achieve Goal 4 of the UN SDG of “Quality Education”, it is important to improve the quality of teachers. According to Mohd Rahimi A Rahman and Mohammad Faiz Ikhlas (2022) who cited the “Pelan Pembangunan Pembelajaran Malaysia 2015” or 2015 Malaysian Education Blueprint (MEB), only 55.1% of teachers achieved the rating of excellence when assessed with the Unified Instrument Assessment (UI or PBPPP), this is not enough to meet the demands of the increasingly fast progress of the world, and in the 2022 MEB, the percentage of teachers that achieved an excellent level in HOTs-based Teaching and Learning is 43.35%. Both numbers are of different scales of assessment, yet both are needed to be considered. To meet the demands of Goal 4 of the UN SDG, it is vital that the training and development of teachers and teaching personnel is improved.

To improve upon this symptom in the current education landscape, it is important for the government to first improve the accessibility of education across the country through the construction of new educational infrastructure in rural lands and/or providing better access to the internet through the construction of more connection towers for an easier access to online learning, and secondly through improving the education provided to the educational staff and to increase the rigidity of the training to provide a much more professional teaching force. The product of implementing these suggestions is a much more discipline and able-bodied line of teachers, and the total educational coverage for all citizens without accounting of their gender, race and religion.

Firstly, the construction of new educational infrastructure and building communication towers in rural areas not only provides the citizens without access to education to the wider world, but it also improves the citizens connect ability. By building new infrastructures, we increase the need for jobs, slowly decreasing the unemployment rate, and by building communication towers, we slowly increase the access to information and increase information literacy, all of this slowly builds up to Malaysia achieving all the SDG goals. However, this suggestion is not without its obstacles, the construction of all the infrastructures requires an exorbitant amount of funds that can range from 100 million ringgit all the way to a billion, depending on the scale. This of course, with it being a rural development project, logistic issues also come into play when it comes to transporting building materials into a large rainforest area, where the most reliable method of transportation is either by foot or by boat. That is not to say that it is not a worthy sacrifice for the future, everything is possible if everyone joined hands to manifest this dream into a reality.

Secondly, the gradual improvement of education provided to the teaching staff and the implementation of a much more rigid and rigorous curriculum and grading system for a much more professional teaching force. By improving the quality given to the teachers, the quality of the students will improve also, by that logic, it is safe to assume that the more amazing a teacher is, the students will follow suit. By providing the teachers a better education and training, they can provide their students much better guidance. The con of this suggestion is that it will increase the stress on the teachers and could possibly decrease the number of teachers in the country, which in on itself a very dangerous thing. To counter that, it is vital for the government to appreciate teachers more by providing them a different pay grade that is higher than the same class of qualification and governmental grade in a non-educational body or agency or extra compensation in the form of quarterly or bi-annually bonuses based on their assessment results.

When both were implemented on a national level, the quality of graduates will force the nation to not only progress in a much better way, but the nation will also achieve the standing of a well-developed nation. Through the increase in information literacy, and the increase in educational participation across all the citizens backgrounds, the SDG goals of developing into a self-sufficient, self-reliant and developed nation is not a fantasy. With more educated citizens it is not unrealistic for our nation to be above the clouds when it comes to our domestic happiness level. By providing quality education, the nation will progress and continue to improve, with the increase in GDP, turning the country into a country with 0% unemployment rate, and turning the nation that has no need for prisons. The products of the country will carry forward and provide a much brighter day for all the citizens, making us a strong force against the tide of time and international conflicts.

Education is not only for the young, and the new, and the rich, and the upper class, it is for everyone, no matter their background, race, religion, gender, or caste. It is for everyone; the world requires us to act against the injustice of not allowing education to become something accessible for everyone. The times will be tough, and tougher, the flow of time stops for no one, and if we do not act now to provide everyone in our beautiful nation the chance to receive a formal education, then we are no different than those who chose not to help those in need. Education is the continuance of life, without it, we are as good as the extinct animals that we killed off in our dance against nature. The consequences may not come now, may not come tomorrow, but mark my words, if education is not vital now, in the next 100 years we will be bound to repeat the same mistakes as our ancestors did hundreds of years ago.
